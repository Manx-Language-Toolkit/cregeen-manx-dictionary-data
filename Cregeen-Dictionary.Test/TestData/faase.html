faase, a. faint, feeble, weak, infirm, not strong.
s’faase, a. how weak, slender, faint. F
faase-rea, s. m. a tup that has been only half castrated.
follym‑faase, a. desolate; Jer. xxv. 38: T’eh er jeet magh ass e ooig, myr lion; son ta’n cheer oc follym faase, kyndagh rish dewilys yn tranlaasagh, as eulys e yymmoose. He hath forsaken his covert, as the lion: for their land is desolate because of the fierceness of the oppressor, and because of his fierce anger; Acts. i. [2]0: Son te scruit ayns lioar ny psalmyn, Lhig da’n ynnyd-vaghee echey ve follym faase, as ny lhig da dooinney erbee cummal ayn: as, Yn aspickys echey lhig da fer elley y ghoaill. For it is written in the book of Psalms, Let his habitation be desolate, and let no man dwell therein: and his bishoprick let another take.
